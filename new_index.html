About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science at Concordia University, where I started my research journey in January 2024. My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Cross-Reality (XR).

My current work investigates the future of work in immersive environments. I explore how Large Language Models (LLMs) and Hybrid Interfaces can be integrated into XR to support complex productivity workflows and creative design, bridging the gap between spatial computing and conventional desktop environments. My earlier doctoral work focused on improving reliability in VR through error mitigation in hand-tracking systems.

Prior to my Ph.D., I completed my B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronics Engineering at Kadir Has University (2022) as the top-ranking student (3.94/4.00 GPA). I also serve as a Teaching Assistant for Game Development courses and have established a strong publication record.

Research Interests

  • Future Work in XR: Designing systems that bridge the gap between immersive headsets and desktop computers for high-utility tasks.
  • Human-AI Collaboration: Exploring the role of Intelligent Assistants (LLMs) in spatial computing and productivity.
  • Virtual Reality (VR) Reliability: Identifying and mitigating hand-tracking failures to create robust, user-friendly interaction methods.
  • Game Development: AI-modelled assets and Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) prototypes.
